This summary captures key points from Jan Hus's sermon on Flower Sunday (Palm Sunday), focusing on Christ's humility, mercy, and justice as He entered Jerusalem.

### Introduction
Jan Hus reflects on Jesus' triumphal entry into Jerusalem, emphasizing His humility, mercy, and justice. This sermon draws lessons from the Gospel accounts, urging believers to follow Christ's example in their lives.

### Key Points
- **Humility of Christ**: Jesus rode on a donkey, not a horse or chariot, to demonstrate humility and reject worldly pride (Matthew 21:1-9, Zechariah 9:9).
  - Hus contrasts this with the pride of church leaders who ride luxuriously on horses.
- **Mercy of Christ**: Jesus wept for Jerusalem, foreseeing its destruction (Luke 19:41-42).
  - He healed the blind and lame in the temple, showing compassion.
- **Justice of Christ**: Jesus drove out the money changers, restoring the temple as a house of prayer (Matthew 21:13).
- **Call to Imitation**: Hus urges believers to emulate Christ's humility, mercy, and justice in their daily lives.
- **Critique of Church Leaders**: Hus condemns the pride and corruption of church officials, contrasting their behavior with Christ's example.
- **Spiritual Lessons**:
  - The donkey symbolizes humility and the removal of sins.
  - Believers are called to cast off old sins and follow Christ with joy and praise.

### Key Quote
> "Dóm mój dóm modlitebný bude nazván, a vy ste jej učinili jeskyní lotrovú."  
> *"My house shall be called a house of prayer, but you have made it a den of thieves."*  
> (Matthew 21:13)
