This is a summary of Jan Hus's sermon on John chapter 16, verses 23-30, focusing on prayer, faith, and salvation. Jan Hus emphasizes the certainty of answered prayer when done in Jesus' name and outlines proper Christian conduct in seeking God's will.

### Key Points:
1. **Answered Prayer**:  
   - Jan Hus teaches that prayers made in Jesus' name will be answered by God the Father.  
   - He stresses the importance of praying for things that lead to salvation, not worldly desires.  

2. **Proper Prayer**:
   - Prayers must be made by righteous individuals, with faith, for worthy causes, through Jesus, and addressed to God.  
   - Jan Hus warns against praying for selfish or harmful things, such as wealth or revenge.  

3. **Jesus as Mediator**:  
   - Christ serves as the mediator between believers and God, ensuring that prayers are heard.  
   - Jan Hus criticizes those who focus on earthly mediators like saints or popes instead of praying directly to God.

4. **The Role of Faith**:  
   - Belief in Christ's divine origin and mission is essential to effective prayer.  
   - Jan Hus highlights the importance of trusting in God's will, even when prayers are not immediately answered.  

5. **Spiritual Growth**:  
   - The sermon encourages believers to grow in faith and understanding of God’s word, moving beyond superficial interpretations.  

### Key Quote:
> "Truly, truly, I say to you, whatever you ask the Father in my name, he will give it to you." – John 16:23
