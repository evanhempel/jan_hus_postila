# Summary of Jan Hus's Sermon on Luke Chapter 19, Verses 41-47

This sermon by Jan Hus focuses on Jesus' lament over Jerusalem and His cleansing of the temple, drawing lessons for believers. It emphasizes Jesus' mercy, truth, and justice, warning against hypocrisy and sin.

## Key Points:
1. Jesus wept over Jerusalem, foreseeing its destruction by Titus and Vespasian because the city failed to recognize God's visitation.
2. He prophesied Jerusalem's devastation, fulfilled 42 years later when the Romans destroyed the city and its inhabitants.
3. Jesus cleansed the temple, driving out merchants and condemning its misuse as a "den of thieves."
4. Hus warns against:
   - Spiritual blindness and failing to recognize God's work.
   - Hypocrisy among religious leaders who prioritize wealth over righteousness.
   - The dangers of sin and its consequences.
5. Believers are called to:
   - Repent of their sins and weep over the sins of others.
   - Guard against greed and misuse of God's house.
   - Remain faithful to God's truth, even in the face of persecution.

**Key Quote**:
> "My house shall be called a house of prayer, but you have made it a den of thieves."
