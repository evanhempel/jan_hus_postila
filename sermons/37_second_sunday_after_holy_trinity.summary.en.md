## Introduction  
Jan Hus's sermon for the Second Sunday After Holy Trinity, based on Luke 14:16-24, explores the parable of a great banquet prepared by Christ. Hus emphasizes God's generosity, the urgency of responding to His call, and the consequences of rejecting His invitation. Below is a concise summary of the key points from this sermon.

## Key Content  
- **The Banquet**:  
  - Christ prepares a "great feast" symbolizing eternal joy and salvation.  
  - The invitation is extended to all, representing God's universal call to salvation.  
- **Three Excuses**:  
  1. A man buys a field and cannot attend (symbolizing pride and worldly ambition).  
  2. Another buys oxen and must test them (symbolizing greed and materialism).  
  3. A third marries a wife and cannot come (symbolizing lust and worldly pleasures).  
- **The Master's Response**:  
  - The master commands his servant to invite the poor, lame, blind, and outcasts, ensuring the banquet is filled.  
  - Hus highlights the grace extended to the lowly and rejected, contrasting it with the excuses of the proud and worldly.  
- **Christ's Warning**:  
  - Those who reject the invitation will not taste the banquet, stressing the finality of their decision.  
  - Hus emphasizes the importance of responding to God's call with urgency and sincerity.  
- **Application**:  
  - Hus critiques the pride, greed, and lust that prevent people from accepting God's invitation.  
  - He calls for repentance and a turn toward eternal joy, warning against delaying salvation.
