This is a summary of Jan Hus's sermon on the Second Sunday of Lent, focusing on the story of the Canaanite woman from the Gospel of Matthew, chapter 15, verses 21-28. Jan Hus emphasizes themes of faith, persistence, humility, and divine mercy.

### Key Points:
1. **The Canaanite Woman's Faith**:
   - A pagan woman from Canaan seeks Jesus’ help for her demon-possessed daughter.
   - Despite initial silence and rejection, she persists in her plea, demonstrating unwavering faith.

2. **Jesus’ Response**:
   - Jesus initially refuses, stating He was sent only to the lost sheep of Israel.
   - The woman humbly accepts being compared to a dog, arguing even dogs eat crumbs from their master's table.

3. **The Lesson**:
   - Jan Hus highlights the woman’s humility, persistence, and faith as a model for believers.
   - Jesus ultimately praises her faith and heals her daughter, showing that God’s mercy extends to all who believe.

### Key Quote:
> "O ženo! veliká jest viera tvá, bud tobě jakož chceš."  
> *(Translation: "O woman! Great is your faith, let it be done for you as you desire.")*
