This is a summary of Jan Hus's sermon on John chapter 20, verses 19-31, focusing on the appearances of Jesus to His disciples after His resurrection, particularly the encounter with Thomas. Jan Hus emphasizes the themes of faith, the Holy Spirit, and the authority to forgive sins.

### Key Points:
1. **Jesus' Appearance and Peace**: Jesus appears to His disciples, offering them peace and showing His wounds to confirm His resurrection.
2. **The Gift of the Holy Spirit**: Jesus breathes on the disciples, granting them the Holy Spirit and the authority to forgive sins.
3. **Thomas' Doubt and Faith**: Thomas doubts until he sees and touches Jesus' wounds, leading to his declaration of faith.
4. **Blessing for Believers Without Sight**: Jesus blesses those who believe without seeing, emphasizing the value of faith.
5. **Authority to Forgive Sins**: 
   - True authority to forgive sins lies only with God.
   - Priests act as instruments, but forgiveness is ultimately granted by God.

### Key Quote:
> "Blessed are those who have not seen and yet have believed." (John 20:29)
