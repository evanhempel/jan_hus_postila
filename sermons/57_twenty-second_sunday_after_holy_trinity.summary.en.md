This is a summary of Jan Hus's sermon based on Matthew 18:23-35, known as the Parable of the Unforgiving Servant. Hus emphasizes the importance of forgiveness and mercy, warning against the dangers of holding grudges and failing to forgive others.

### Key Points:
1. **The Parable's Lesson**: Jan Hus explains that the parable illustrates the necessity of forgiving others, as God forgives us.
2. **God's Mercy**: The king forgives the servant's enormous debt, symbolizing God's boundless mercy toward sinners.
3. **The Servant's Failure**: The forgiven servant refuses to show mercy to a fellow servant who owed him a small amount, demonstrating hypocrisy.
4. **Consequences of Unforgiveness**: The unforgiving servant is punished, representing the severe consequences of failing to forgive others.
5. **Application to Life**: Hus urges listeners to forgive others sincerely, as God will judge them according to their willingness to forgive.
6. **Warning to the Unmerciful**: Those who refuse to forgive will face eternal punishment, just as the unforgiving servant did.
