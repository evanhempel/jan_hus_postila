This summary covers Jan Hus's sermon for the Fourth Sunday of Lent, focusing on the Gospel account of Jesus feeding the five thousand from John chapter 6. The sermon emphasizes spiritual nourishment and the importance of repentance.

### Key Content:
- **Context:** Jan Hus begins by recounting the miracle of Jesus feeding 5,000 people with five loaves and two fish, as described in John 6:1-14.
- **Spiritual Meaning:** The miracle symbolizes spiritual nourishment, where Christ feeds His faithful with His Word and grace.
- **Call to Repentance:** Jan Hus highlights the importance of repentance, especially during Lent, and encourages believers to find joy in God’s mercy.
- **Preparation for Easter:** The sermon connects the miracle to the approaching Easter, urging Christians to prepare their hearts for the celebration of Christ’s resurrection.
- **Symbolism of Bread and Fish:** The five loaves represent five spiritual teachings: fear of eternal damnation, remembrance of death, turning from sin, sorrow for sins, and the resolve not to sin. The two fish symbolize prayer and fasting.
- **Example of Christ:** Jan Hus draws parallels between Christ’s actions and His followers’ lives, emphasizing humility and following Christ’s example.

### Key Quote:
> "Raduj sě, Jeruzaléme! To jest, ty duše, jenž čakáš radosti nebeského Jeruzaléma; neb raduj sě, Jeruzaléme! To jest, ty sboře křěstanský, jenž již hřiechóv pozbyv, máš chlebem od Krista, to věz, jeho svatým tělem krmen a nasycen býti."  
> ("Rejoice, Jerusalem! That is, you soul, who awaits the joy of heavenly Jerusalem; rejoice, Jerusalem! That is, you Christian assembly, having now put aside sins, will be fed and satisfied with the bread from Christ, that is, His holy body.")
