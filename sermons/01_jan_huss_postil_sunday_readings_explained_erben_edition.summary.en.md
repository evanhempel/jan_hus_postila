This is a summary of the introductory material to Jan Hus's *Postila*, a collection of his Czech sermons written during his exile in 1413. The full text provides historical context about the work's creation, its significance in Hus's life, and its impact on Czech religious reform.

### Key Content:
- **Historical Context**:
  - Written during Hus's exile at Kozí Hrádek (1413), this is his final major work.
  - Compiled from earlier Latin sermons, adapted for lay audiences.
  - Reflects Hus's defiance against church corruption, especially simony and clerical wealth.

- **Themes**:
  1. **Reformist Theology**: Hus condemns clerical greed, advocating poverty for priests (inspired by John Wycliffe and Milíč of Kroměříž).
  2. **Defiance of Authority**: Defends his excommunication and critiques papal abuses.
  3. **Moral Urgency**: Calls for a return to apostolic simplicity, equating church wealth with spiritual poison.

- **Legacy**:
  - Influenced the Hussite Revolution, including the seizure of church property.
  - Hus's martyrdom (1415) followed shortly after completing the *Postila*.

### Key Quote:
> *"Truth conquers all things and endures forever."*  
> *(Hus’s rallying cry, echoing his defiance against ecclesiastical oppression.)*
