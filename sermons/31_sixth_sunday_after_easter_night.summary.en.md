Jan Hus's sermon on the Sixth Sunday after Easter focuses on the coming of the Holy Spirit as the Comforter, the trials faced by Christ's followers, and the importance of enduring hardships with faith. He emphasizes that the Holy Spirit will guide believers to truth and strengthen them against persecution.

**Key Points**:
  1. **The Holy Spirit as Comforter**: Jan Hus explains that the Holy Spirit, sent by Jesus and the Father, will comfort the sorrowful and testify to the truth of Christ’s teachings (John 15:26-27).
  2. **Persecution Endured by Believers**: Hus highlights that followers of Christ will face hatred, excommunication, and even death, but these challenges are ultimately beneficial for salvation (John 16:1-4).
  3. **The Role of Endurance**: He encourages believers to endure suffering patiently, reminding them that Christ foretold these trials to prepare them (John 16:1-4).
  4. **The Holy Spirit’s Work Against Sin**: The Holy Spirit confronts sin, revives the spiritually dead, and exposes lies, guiding believers toward eternal truth (John 16:7-11).
  5. **The Importance of Truth**: Hus stresses that the Holy Spirit, as the Spirit of Truth, will equip believers to testify to Christ, even in the face of opposition (John 15:26).

**Key Quote**:
  > "When the Comforter comes, whom I will send to you from the Father, the Spirit of truth who proceeds from the Father, He will testify about me." (John 15:26)
