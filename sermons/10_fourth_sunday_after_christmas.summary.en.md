This sermon by Jan Hus focuses on the Gospel reading from Matthew chapter 8, verses 1-13, which recounts Jesus healing a leper and a centurion's servant. Hus uses these miracles to teach about faith, humility, and the power of Christ. Below is a summary of the key points.

### Key Content:
- **Jesus heals a leper**: 
  - The leper approaches Jesus, saying, "Lord, if you are willing, you can make me clean" (Matthew 8:2). 
  - Jesus touches the leper, demonstrating His willingness to heal even those considered unclean.
  - Hus emphasizes that leprosy symbolizes sin, and only Christ can cleanse the soul from spiritual leprosy.
- **Healing of the centurion's servant**:
  - The centurion, a Gentile, shows deep faith, believing Jesus can heal his servant with just a word (Matthew 8:8).
  - Jesus praises the centurion's faith, stating He has not found such great faith in Israel (Matthew 8:10).
  - Hus highlights the centurion’s humility and faith as examples for believers.
- **Spiritual lessons**:
  - Christ’s miracles prove His divine power and mercy, calling believers to trust in Him.
  - Hus warns against pride, especially among clergy, who often seek recognition for their works rather than glorifying God.
  - The sermon also warns of eternal punishment for those who reject Christ, contrasting it with the reward of faith.

### Key Quote:
> "Lord, if you are willing, you can make me clean." - *Matthew 8:2*
