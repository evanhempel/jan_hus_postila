This is a summary of Jan Hus's sermon on Luke 16:19-31, focusing on the parable of the rich man and Lazarus. Hus uses this passage to warn against the dangers of pride, luxury, and lack of mercy, contrasting the eternal destinies of the rich man and Lazarus.

### Key Content:
- **The Parable**: Jesus tells of a rich man who lives luxuriously while neglecting the poor beggar Lazarus at his gate.
- **After Death**: Lazarus is carried to Abraham's bosom, while the rich man suffers torment in hell.
- **Rich Man's Plea**: The rich man begs Abraham to send Lazarus to cool his tongue, but Abraham replies that the chasm between them is fixed.
- **Warning to the Living**: Hus emphasizes the importance of humility, mercy, and detachment from earthly wealth.

### Key Quote:
> "Remember, son, that you received your good things in your lifetime, and Lazarus likewise received evil things; but now he is comforted, and you are tormented."  
> - Abraham to the rich man (Luke 16:25)
