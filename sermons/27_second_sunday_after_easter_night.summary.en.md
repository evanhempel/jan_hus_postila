Jan Hus's sermon, based on John chapter 10, verses 11-16, emphasizes the qualities of a good shepherd, contrasting Christ as the true shepherd with false church leaders. This summary highlights key insights from the sermon, focusing on the responsibilities of spiritual leaders and the dangers of neglecting their duties.

- **Key Themes**:
  1. **Christ as the Good Shepherd**: Jesus is the ultimate shepherd who lays down His life for His sheep, unlike hirelings who flee when danger arises.
  2. **False Leaders**: Church leaders who prioritize personal gain over spiritual care are likened to hirelings and are condemned.
  3. **Duties of a Shepherd**: A true shepherd must provide nourishment, protect the flock from harm, and heal the sick, mirroring Christ’s actions.
  4. **Condemnation of Neglect**: Leaders who fail to teach or correct their flock will face divine judgment.
  5. **Unity in Christ**: All believers, both Jews and Gentiles, will ultimately unite under one shepherd—Christ.

**Key Quote**:
> "I am the good shepherd. The good shepherd lays down his life for the sheep."  
> —John 10:11
