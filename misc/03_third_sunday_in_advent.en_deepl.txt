26 Reading 3.  
READING FOR THE THIRD SUNDAY OF ADVENT.  
St. Matthew, chapter 11, verses 2-10.  
At that time, when John heard in prison the works of  
Christ, he sent two of his disciples to him  (Jesus), saying, "Are you the one who is to come [to redeem], or  shall we look for another?" And Jesus answered and said unto them [John's disciples]: Go and tell John those things which ye have heard and seen: the blind see [being healed], the lame walk, the lepers are cleansed, the deaf hear, the dead are raised, the poor have the gospel preached to them [evangelisantur, that is, they hear the word of God and are saved by it]; and blessed is he who is not offended [does not believe in me. And when they had gone, Jesus began to speak to the crowd about John:  What did you go out into the wilderness to see? A reed shaken by the wind  (that is, is there no man whose pride has shaken him or  perseverance)? And what did you go out to see? A man dressed in soft clothing [but he walks in a hair shirt]? Behold, those who dress in soft clothing are in kings' houses [as proud and luxurious]. And what did you go out to see? A prophet? But I tell you, more than for a year; this is surely the one about whom it is written [Malachi the prophet writes in 3:1: Behold, I will send my messenger (t. messenger: angelus in Greek, messenger in Czech) before your face, who will prepare your way before you.
